## Cultural Conversion Cases

### 1. The Concept of Family
**Chinese Context**: Three generations under one roof, with family honor being paramount.
**Localization**:
- Emphasize individual choice and freedom.
- Convert family ties to emotional support.
- Reduce the depiction of absolute obedience to elders.

### 2. The Culture of "Face"
**Chinese Context**: Maintaining face, avoiding direct conflict.
**Localization**:
- Convert to personal dignity and self-esteem.
- Express conflict and confrontation directly.
- Emphasize the value of honesty and straightforwardness.

### 3. Collectivism
**Chinese Context**: The interests of the collective are higher than those of the individual.
**Localization**:
- Balance individual and collective interests.
- Emphasize teamwork rather than sacrifice.
- Highlight the value of individual contributions.

## Content Optimization Suggestions

### Opening Optimization
1. **Strengthen the Hook**
   - The first sentence must grab the reader.
   - Immediately present a conflict or suspense.
   - Avoid a lengthy background introduction.

2. **Simplify the World-building**
   - Gradually reveal the world setting.
   - Show the rules through action.
   - Avoid information overload.

### Westernization of Characters
1. **Personality Traits**
   - More independent and autonomous.
   - Express emotions directly.
   - Have a distinctive and unique personality.

2. **Dialogue Style**
   - Use contemporary colloquialisms.
   - Add elements of humor.
   - Reduce polite formalities.

### Plot Adjustment
1. **Pacing Control**
   - Faster plot advancement.
   - Reduce inner monologues.
   - Increase dialogue and action.

2. **Conflict Design**
   - External conflict is better than internal struggle.
   - A clear opposition.
   - Clear standards for victory and defeat.
