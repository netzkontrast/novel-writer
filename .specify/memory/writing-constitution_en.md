# Writing Constitution

## Core Creative Philosophy

### 1. Story First
**Content is king, driven by plot**
- Every chapter must advance the story.
- Avoid meaningless daily descriptions.
- Conflict and tension are the lifeblood of the story.
- Keep the reader always wanting to read more.

### 2. Three-Dimensional Characters
**The depth of characterization determines the height of the work**
- Every character has their own motivations and goals.
- Character actions must be consistent with their personality settings.
- Show characters through actions and dialogue, not direct descriptions.
- Supporting characters must also be flesh and blood, not just tools.

### 3. Consistent Worldview
**The rationality and integrity of the setting**
- Establish clear rules for how the world operates.
- Once a setting is established, it must remain consistent.
- Avoid changing settings at will for the sake of the plot.
- The worldview serves the story, it doesn't steal the show.

### 4. Refined Language
**The power of words lies in precision, not flourish**
- Convey the most information with the fewest words.
- Avoid excessive use of adjectives and adverbs.
- Dialogue must match the character's identity and personality.
- Descriptions should be vivid, making the reader feel present.

### 5. Authentic Emotions
**Only genuine emotions can touch people's hearts**
- Emotional development must have logic and a process.
- Avoid abrupt emotional turns.
- Express emotions through details, not direct preaching.
- The highest goal is to make the reader resonate.

## Writing Specifications

### Principles to Follow
1.  **Engaging Opening** - The first 3 paragraphs of each chapter must have a hook.
2.  **Complete Chapters** - Each chapter is a relatively complete short story.
3.  **Foreshadowing Payoff** - Foreshadowing must have a corresponding reveal.
4.  **Logical Self-Consistency** - Plot development must follow causal logic.
5.  **Pacing Control** - A good balance of tension and release, avoiding constant tension or dragging.

### Problems to Avoid
1.  **Water-padding** - Meaningless repetition and procrastination.
2.  **Character Collapse** - Character actions contradict their settings.
3.  **Forced Turns** - Sudden reversals without foreshadowing.
4.  **Excessive Preaching** - Directly instilling values.
5.  **Clich√© Overload** - Overuse of old tropes.

### Style Requirements
- **Narrative Language**: Concise and smooth, avoiding lengthy sentences.
- **Dialogue Style**: Natural and lively, consistent with the character's identity.
- **Descriptive Techniques**: Highlight key points, with appropriate detail.
- **Rhetorical Devices**: Used appropriately, not excessively.

## Creative Process

### Phase 1: Conception and Preparation
1.  Clarify the story's theme and core conflict.
2.  Design a complete story framework.
3.  Create detailed character profiles.
4.  Build a self-consistent worldview system.

### Phase 2: Outline Planning
1.  Determine the overall direction of the story.
2.  Plan the main content of each volume.
3.  Design the transitions between chapters.
4.  Mark important turning points and climaxes.

### Phase 3: Content Creation
1.  Write chapter by chapter according to the outline.
2.  Maintain a stable update rhythm.
3.  Regularly review previously written content.
4.  Adjust subsequent plans in a timely manner.

### Phase 4: Revision and Improvement
1.  Check the logicality of the plot.
2.  Unify the writing style and wording.
3.  Perfect the detailed descriptions.
4.  Optimize the dialogue content.

## Quality Standards

### Standards for an Excellent Chapter
- **Engaging Opening** - Quickly grabs the reader's attention.
- **Substantial Middle** - The plot progresses with momentum.
- **Powerful Ending** - Leaves a cliffhanger or impact.
- **Full of Emotion** - Can touch the reader's emotions.
- **Reasonable Pacing** - A good balance of tension and release, without dragging.

### Standards for a Finished Work
- **Clear Theme** - Conveys a clear core idea.
- **Complete Structure** - Beginning, development, climax, and ending are all present.
- **Character Growth** - The main characters show significant change.
- **Conflict Resolution** - The main conflicts are resolved reasonably.
- **Lasting Impression** - Leaves the reader with something to think about after finishing.

## Continuous Improvement

### Directions for Self-Improvement
1.  Read a wide range of excellent works to learn from others' strengths.
2.  Experience life deeply to accumulate creative material.
3.  Study writing techniques to enhance professional skills.
4.  Accept reader feedback to understand market demands.
5.  Maintain creative passion and constantly break through personal limits.

### Key Points for Regular Review
- Which chapters received the best response, and why?
- Which plot points were not handled ideally?
- Was the characterization successful?
- Was the pacing control appropriate?
- What content are the readers most concerned about?
