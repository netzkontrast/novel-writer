## Vocabulary Selection Preferences
- **Common Word Substitutions**
  - Say "so" or "so that's how" instead of "therefore."
  - Say "but" or "but, well" instead of "however."
  - Say "especially," "really," or "quite" instead of "very."
  - Say "maybe," "perhaps," or "it's possible" instead of "perhaps."
- **Quantifier Habits**
  - a person / an individual, a dog / one dog
  - a meal / one meal, a rain / a shower of rain
- **Adjective Tastes**
  - Use "good-looking" or "pretty" more than "beautiful."
  - Use "not bad" or "pretty good" more than "excellent."
  - Avoid formal adjectives, prefer colloquial ones.

## Sentence Construction Habits
- **Habitual Openings**
  - Well, let me tell you, you know?
  - Seriously, to be honest, truthfully.
  - If you ask me, in my opinion, let's think of it this way.
- **Mid-sentence Fillers**
  - I mean, you know, how should I say it?
  - Right? Isn't it? What do you think?
  - This thing, you know, anyway.
- **Ending Particles**
  - ba, a, ne, ma, ha
  - de, le, zhe, guo (preference for dynamic auxiliary words)
  - Habit of using ellipsis (... or ...)

## Natural Imperfections (Important: to increase authenticity)
- **Occasional Typos** (1-2 per thousand characters)
  - Confusion of "de," "di," "de": "He happily running."
  - Homophone errors: writing "at where" instead of "in where."
- **Punctuation Randomness**
  - Inconsistent number of ellipses: ... or ... or ......
  - Casual use of commas and periods.
  - Occasionally forgetting punctuation, especially in dialogue.
- **Colloquial Omissions**
  - Saying "but" instead of "however."
  - Saying "like this" instead of "this way."
  - Subject omission: "Feel this is not reliable."
- **Imperfect Grammar**
  - Occasional word order adjustments: "This thing I really didn't expect."
  - Incomplete sentences: "If only I had known earlier..."
  - Repetitive emphasis: "Really, really important."
